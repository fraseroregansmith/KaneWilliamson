A bit about Kane Williamson


Early Career

Kane Williamson was born on 8 August 1990 in Tauranga, New Zealand. As well as his father Brett, and his mother Sandra, he has 3 older sister sand a twin brother. His love for cricket was most likely passed on through his dad Brett who was a very keen cricketer when he was younger.

He attended Tauranga Boys College between 2004 and 2008, and he was even head boy in 2008. His first class career actually started while he was at school, he made his debut for Northern Districts on December 10, 2007 at the age of 17.

In 2008 he captained the New Zealand under 19's to the semi-final in the under 19 World cup in Malaysia

Building his career

After good domestic success, he earned the callup for the blackcaps to play India in an ODI on the 10th of August, 2010. Unfortunatly he got a duck.

He earned his test callup against India in Ahmedabad on the 4th of November, 2010 after he had good succes in an ODI series againt bangaldesh, inculding a 108. On his Test debut, he got 131 and became the 8th new zealander to get a Test century on debut, and the 91st in the world.

World Class



Centuries, and match winning preformances from Williamson were becoming more frequent. in 2014 & 2015, he scored 9 test centuries,including an amazing 242* against Sri Lanka in Wellington. He also scored 5 ODI centuries in 2015, solidifying his place as one of the best in the world.

Taking The Captaincy



In early 2016 after Brendon McCullum retired, the captaincy was passed onto Kane. He is now a world class captain, as shown how he was the captain of the 2021 Icc mens test team of the year. He is New Zealands most succesful captain, taking us to the 2019 CWC final, Champions of the inaugural Test championship, and the final of the 2021 T20I world cup. He is now considered one of the best captains and batsmen of this age. He was captian of the ICC mens test team of the year 2021
